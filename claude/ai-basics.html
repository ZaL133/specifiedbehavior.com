AI Basics

The fundamentals of artificial intelligence — concepts, history, and key milestones.

What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science that aims to create machines capable of mimicking human intelligence. This can involve learning, reasoning, problem-solving, perception, and language understanding.

Types of AI

AI can be classified into two main types: Narrow AI, designed to perform a specific task (such as facial recognition or internet searches), and General AI, which can theoretically perform any intellectual task that a human being can.

Machine Learning

Machine Learning (ML) is a subset of AI. With ML, computers learn by discovering patterns and insights from data rather than following explicitly programmed rules. There are two primary approaches: supervised learning (learning from labeled examples) and unsupervised learning (finding patterns in unlabeled data).

Deep Learning

Deep Learning is a subset of Machine Learning that uses neural networks with many layers — hence the "deep." These layers are responsible for recognizing and extracting features from input data, enabling breakthroughs in image recognition, speech, and language.

Neural Networks

An Artificial Neural Network (ANN) is modeled after the human brain and designed to mimic how humans learn. It can adapt to changing input, generating the best possible result without requiring the output criteria to be explicitly redesigned.

Natural Language Processing

Natural Language Processing (NLP) refers to AI methods of communicating with intelligent systems using natural language. AI can interpret, recognize, and generate human language in useful ways — powering everything from search engines to conversational assistants.

Computer Vision

Computer Vision is the science of enabling computers to recognize and understand images and scenes. It's the foundation of many AI advances, particularly in recognizing patterns and objects in images and video.

A Brief History of AI

  • 1950

    Alan Turing proposed the concept of machines that could simulate any human intelligence — widely recognized as the beginning of AI as a field.

  • 1956

    The term "Artificial Intelligence" was coined at the Dartmouth Conference, the first AI conference.

  • 1960s–70s

    Early AI research focused on problem-solving and symbolic methods with human-written rules.

  • 1980s

    Machine Learning emerged as a new paradigm, focusing on algorithms that could learn from and make predictions based on data.

  • 1990s

    Practical achievements in data mining and speech recognition. The web generated vast amounts of training data.

  • 2011

    IBM's Watson won Jeopardy!, demonstrating AI's ability to understand and respond to natural language queries.

  • 2012

    A deep learning breakthrough: Geoffrey Hinton's team won the ImageNet object recognition competition, igniting the deep learning era.

  • 2016

    Google's AlphaGo beat the world champion Go player, a milestone long thought to be decades away.

  • 2018

    OpenAI released GPT-2, a language model capable of generating coherent, contextually relevant text.

  • 2020

    OpenAI released GPT-3 — 175 billion parameters, capable of translation, question answering, and creative writing.

  • 2022–2023

    ChatGPT launched to the public, reaching 100 million users in two months. The AI era for mainstream users had begun.

  • 2024–2025

    AI reasoning models (o1, o3, Claude 3.5) and multimodal systems became widely available, enabling complex problem-solving across images, code, and language.

Interesting Facts

  • AI can learn from experience — this is called machine learning, and it's how Netflix recommends movies you might enjoy.
  • Some AI can recognize and respond to emotions. This is called affective computing, used in customer service bots and accessibility tools.
  • AI is used in healthcare to predict patient outcomes and help doctors make faster, more accurate diagnoses.
  • Despite science fiction, we're still far from AI that truly mimics human intelligence. Current AI excels at specific tasks but struggles with context and generalization.
  • AI can be creative — it has been used to compose music, create visual art, and write stories and poetry.
  • AI is a major driver in autonomous vehicles, processing complex sensor data to navigate city streets in real time.
  • AI helps fight climate change by optimizing energy use, predicting weather for renewable sources, and monitoring deforestation.
  • Large language models like GPT-4 and Claude were trained on hundreds of billions of words from the internet, books, and code.
  • The transformer architecture, introduced by Google in 2017 ("Attention Is All You Need"), is the foundation of virtually every major AI language model today.
